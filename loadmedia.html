President Trump's proposed "$5 million gold card" visa, offering a path to US citizenship, faces criticism for potentially inviting fraud and national security risks. Experts warn of parallels to the EB-5 program's issues, questioning the vetting process's effectiveness.

Canadian Premier Threatens to Cut Off Energy to US in Response to Trump Tariffs

Ontario Premier Doug Ford threatens to cut off energy exports to the US in response to President Trump's tariffs, stating "they need to feel the pain." Canada, a major electricity exporter to the US, particularly to New York, Michigan, and Minnesota, is considering matching US tariffs.

Arab Countries Meet in Egypt to Discuss Alternative Proposal to Trump's Gaza Plan

Arab leaders held an emergency summit in Cairo to present a counterproposal to President Trump's Gaza plan, focusing on Egypt's $53 billion reconstruction blueprint. Houthis Designated as a Foreign Terrorist Organization, Rubio Announces

Secretary of State Marco Rubio announced the designation of the Iran-backed Houthis in Yemen as a foreign terrorist organization, fulfilling one of President Trump's promises. The move aims to protect US national security interests and curtail support for terrorist activities. The Houthis have carried out numerous attacks in the Red Sea.

China Digs Deeper in West Asia, Firm Signs $557 Mn Contract in Kuwait; 15,000 Workers Already in UAE

A Wuhan-based firm, China Gezhouba Group Company Limited (CGGC), signed a $557 million deal with Kuwait's Public Authority for Housing Welfare (PAHW) for infrastructure projects in South Saad Al-Abdullah New City. This marks a significant step in China's expanding presence in West Asia. Additionally, nearly 15,500 Chinese companies are already operating in the UAE.

China to Promote Open-source RISC-V Chips, Boosting Domestic Chip Industry

China plans to release instructions encouraging the nationwide use of open-source RISC-V processors to reduce reliance on Western technology. This move, a collaboration between eight government departments, aims to bolster China's domestic chip industry amidst growing US-China tech tensions.
