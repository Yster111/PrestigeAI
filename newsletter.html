Negotiate with Russia with 'gun on the table,' Lithuanian defense minister says

Lithuanian Defense Minister Dovilė Šakalienė, drawing on her family's experience under Soviet rule, emphasizes the need for a strong stance in negotiations with Russia, likening effective diplomacy to having a "gun on the table." She expresses deep distrust of Putin and highlights President Trump's tough approach as a potential safeguard. Lithuania is significantly increasing defense spending, exceeding NATO targets, and withdrawing from international treaties on cluster munitions and landmines to send a strong message to Russia.

New study challenges narrative that Israel deliberately caused starvation in Gaza

A new study by Israeli public health experts challenges claims of Israeli-caused famine in Gaza, showing that food supplies entering Gaza have met or exceeded humanitarian standards. The study emphasizes that any hunger issues are due to Hamas's control and weaponization of food distribution, not a lack of aid entering the region. The study faced rigorous review due to anti-Israel bias within the scientific community.

Ex-Philippine President Duterte shrugs off possible arrest by ICC for drug war during trip to Hong Kong

Former Philippine President Rodrigo Duterte, during an unannounced visit to Hong Kong, addressed the possibility of an International Criminal Court (ICC) arrest warrant for alleged crimes against humanity during his war on drugs. He stated he would accept his fate if arrested. The Philippine government is prepared for any eventuality, and law enforcement is cooperating with the ICC.

Frontrunner in Romania’s annulled presidential election barred from race

Calin Georgescu, a populist candidate and frontrunner in Romania's previously annulled presidential election, has been banned from running in the re-run. Georgescu decried the decision as a sign of tyranny, while figures like Elon Musk and Kari Lake criticized the move, comparing it to actions against other nationalist leaders. The Trump administration has expressed concern over Romania's election handling.

Trump critic Mark Carney wins Liberal Party nomination, will become Canada's next PM

Former Bank of Canada governor Mark Carney has won the Liberal leadership race and will become Canada's next prime minister. He vowed to maintain tariffs against the United States and asserted Canada's independence from America. Carney's victory sets the stage for a likely snap election against Conservative leader Pierre Poilievre, who has been critical of Trudeau's government.

Chinese espionage group Silk Typhoon has new tactics to target US networks

The Chinese state-sponsored cyber espionage group, Silk Typhoon, has adopted new tactics, including leveraging stolen API keys and exploiting zero-day vulnerabilities, to target US government agencies, businesses, and critical infrastructure. They have been observed infiltrating IT providers, MSPs, and cloud data management firms to gain access to sensitive data and maintain long term access.
